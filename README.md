Hello, you.
Sometimes you don't need a relationship. You just need someone there.

**Why I'm building this**
Honestly, it started with my own drives. I get a little lonely on long stretches behind the wheel, nothing dramatic, just those quiet moments where I wish someone was in the passenger seat to fill the silence. Not a friend I have to call and properly catch up with, not a date. Just a voice for a bit. One night driving home it occurred to me that a lot of people probably feel this exact thing, and not only in the car. Cooking dinner, cleaning the house, studying, lying awake at 2am. There are moments where you don't need anything big, you just don't want to be alone in that moment. I couldn't really find an app made for that, so I started sketching one. That's the whole idea. 'Hello, you.' connects you with one real person for a short voice conversation when you'd rather not be alone. No swiping, no profiles to scroll, no last names. Just twenty minutes of company.

**What it is**
You tell it how you're feeling and what you need. It matches you with someone who's after the same kind of thing, and the two of you have a short voice call. That's basically it.
It's meant for all sorts of moments, the quiet drive, the empty kitchen, the late night, or the good day when something happens and you just want to tell someone.

**What it isn't**
Being clear about this part matters to me. It's not a dating app, and it's not trying to be another social network. No feeds, no likes, no follower counts, none of that. It's also not random anonymous chat where people are throwaway. Everyone has a steady identity and a reputation that comes from how their conversations actually go. And you never swap phone numbers or personal details. The conversation stays inside the app.

**How it works**
The flow is short on purpose. Three taps and you're talking.
  A[Open the app] --> B{How are you feeling?}
  B --> C{What do you need?}
  C --> D[Matching, a calm breathing pulse]
  D --> E[Connected: first name, rating, a few interests]
  E --> F[Twenty minute voice call]
  F --> G{Continue? both people have to agree}
  G -- Yes --> F
  G -- No --> H[Call ends with a gentle recap]
  H --> I[Rate the conversation, just for you]
  I --> J{Talk again? only opens if you both say yes}
The matching is about needs fitting together. Someone who needs to be heard gets paired with someone who likes to listen. Someone with good news gets paired with someone who wants to celebrate. It's not about how anyone looks.

**What I care about**
A few things I want to hold onto as this grows:
It's about company, not dating. That line matters and I don't want it to blur.
People stay anonymous, but not faceless. You get a name, a simple avatar, and a couple of interests. Enough to feel like a real person, without photos or personal info.
The only thing that counts is whether the conversation was good. Were you kind, easy to talk to, a decent listener. No looks, no popularity.
Nobody gets pushed on anyone. To keep talking past twenty minutes you both have to say yes, and you can only reach someone again if you both choose to.
Leaving is always easy. One tap, no explanation, no questions.

**Design**
The look is calm and simple. A dark or a cream background, your choice. Big clean type (SF Pro), lots of space, and one soft lavender accent without much else. No photos, just abstract gradient avatars. I was looking at apps like Notion, Linear, and Monzo for the feel. I wanted it to feel quiet, not busy.

**Safety**
This is a big one for me, partly because it's the right thing and partly because an app that connects strangers has to get it right to even be allowed on the App Store. The plan includes:
Live moderation during calls.
A one tap Report and Leave that ends the call and flags it.
Blocking someone so they never get matched to you again.
Clear community rules against bad behavior, and quick action on reports.
Optional identity verification, shown as a badge, kept private.
Age is hidden by default, since this is about company and not dating.
No unsolicited messages. Someone can only reach you if you both said yes.
